# Shoot Brief Builder

Deliverable: a **shoot brief** — the document that turns "we need product photos" into something a photographer, stylist, or production team can shoot from without a call. It bridges AI-native brand tooling to a real-world production day: cameras, sets, and people, not another generated image. A brief that's just "make it look premium" isn't a brief; it's a mood.

Works with no Jinn connection. Connected to a brand's Jinn Brand DNA, the mood language, palette, and messaging emphasis come from the brand's real system instead of your best guess — see **If a Jinn MCP connection is present**.

## The deliverable

```
SHOOT BRIEF — <product / concept>, deliverables due <date>

Shoot purpose:      <the single job these images do — PDP launch, seasonal refresh, campaign hero>
Deliverable specs:  <formats, resolution, aspect ratios, file naming, # of selects, usage rights, deadline>

SHOT LIST
# | Shot type          | Ratio | Channel          | Framing / angle note
1 | Hero / PDP          | 1:1   | Product page      | ...
2 | Lifestyle / in-use   | 4:5   | Paid social       | ...
3 | Detail / texture     | 1:1   | PDP gallery       | ...
4 | Social square        | 1:1   | Instagram feed    | ...
5 | Social story          | 9:16  | Instagram/TikTok  | ...
6 | Email hero            | 4:5   | Lifecycle email   | ...

LIGHTING & MOOD
Time of day:        <morning window light / golden hour / studio-controlled / overcast diffuse>
Light quality:       <hard/soft, direction, color temperature>
Mood in 3 words:     <the adjectives every shot has to earn>

SET & PROPS
Location / backdrop: ...
Props IN:            ...
Props OUT (avoid):   ...
Styling notes:       ...

CAST / TALENT (if applicable)
Wardrobe:             ...
Expression / energy:  ...

CONTINUITY NOTES
<what has to match shot-to-shot — setting, light, cast, mood — so six shots read as one story, not six unrelated frames>
```

## Procedure (ungrounded — works with no Jinn connection)

### 1. Intake

| Field | Capture |
|-------|---------|
| Product / concept | what's being shot, and the one creative idea behind the shoot |
| Shoot purpose | the single job the images do — launch hero, evergreen PDP, seasonal refresh, campaign key art |
| Deliverable channels | every place these images run (PDP, paid social, email, marketplace, out-of-home) — this drives the shot list, not an afterthought |
| Constraints | budget tier, location access, talent availability, shoot-day count, anything the photographer needs to know before they plan the day |
| Reference | any existing brand imagery, mood boards, or competitor shoots the user wants to match or deliberately break from |

If any field is missing, ask — a brief built on a guessed purpose sends the crew to shoot the wrong thing.

### 2. Build the shot list, keyed by where each image runs

Don't start from "what should we shoot" — start from **every place the images need to run**, then build one shot per placement. A shot list organized by output (channel × aspect ratio) survives contact with production; a shot list organized by "cool ideas" doesn't. Standard e-commerce/brand categories to check against the deliverable channels:

| Shot type | Typical ratio | What it does |
|-----------|---------------|---------------|
| Hero / PDP | 1:1 or 4:5 | The primary product-page image — clean, unambiguous, sells the object itself |
| Lifestyle / in-use | 4:5 or 16:9 | Product in context — shows the *use case*, not just the object |
| Detail / texture | 1:1 | Close crop on material, finish, or a feature that doesn't read at full-shot scale |
| Flat-lay | 1:1 | Overhead composition — common for apparel, food, and multi-item sets |
| Social square / story | 1:1 / 9:16 | Native-feeling crops for feed and stories — not a repurposed hero shot |
| Email hero | 4:5 or 16:9 | Wide enough to sit above the fold with copy overlay room |
| Marketplace / compliance | 1:1, white background | Meets the platform's technical listing requirements — least creative, most load-bearing |

Cut any shot type that doesn't map to a real deliverable channel — a shot list padded with "nice to have" setups is how shoot days run over budget and behind schedule.

### 3. Direct the lighting and mood

Name three things, concretely enough that a photographer can set up without a follow-up call:

- **Time of day / light source** — morning window light, golden hour exterior, studio strobe, overcast diffuse. Vague ("bright and airy") wastes a shoot day; specific ("soft north-facing window light, no fill") doesn't.
- **Light quality** — hard vs. soft, direction (front/side/back), and color temperature (warm/neutral/cool). This is what actually reads as "brand" in a photo — more than the product itself, in most categories.
- **Mood in three words** — the adjectives every single shot has to earn. If a shot doesn't fit the three words, it's the wrong shot, not an exception.

### 4. Set, props, and styling

- **Location / backdrop** — specific enough to scout or build (a described studio cyc color, a real neighborhood, a described interior), not "somewhere nice."
- **Props IN** — the objects that reinforce the story. Name them, don't describe a vibe.
- **Props OUT** — what to actively avoid; this list prevents a stylist's reasonable-but-wrong choice from making it into a shot (a competitor's product, an off-brand color, clutter that fights the hero object).
- **Styling notes** — surface finish, garment steaming/pinning, any physical prep the object needs before it's camera-ready.

### 5. Cast and talent (when the shoot includes people)

Wardrobe (specific pieces/colors, not "casual"), expression and energy (the three mood words, translated to a human face and body), and any diversity/representation brief the brand requires. Skip this section entirely for still-life/product-only shoots.

### 6. Write the continuity notes

This is the section that keeps a multi-shot day from producing six disconnected images. Lock down the same handful of decisions across every shot in the set — the setting, the time of day and light, who's in frame, and the mood — and write them down once so nobody on set has to re-derive them shot to shot. A shoot brief that lets lighting quietly drift from shot 1 to shot 6 produces a gallery that doesn't read as one product, one story, one brand — even if every individual frame is good.

### 7. Set the deliverable specs

File format, resolution, aspect ratios per shot (from the shot list), file naming convention, number of selects/retouched finals expected, usage rights and where the images are licensed to run, and the delivery deadline. This is the section production actually gets held to — don't leave it vague.

### 8. Self-check before delivering

- Every shot in the list traces to a real deliverable channel — none are "cool idea, no home."
- Lighting and mood direction are concrete enough to set up without a follow-up question.
- Props OUT list exists, not just props IN — it's the list that prevents the most common on-set mistake.
- Continuity notes cover setting, light, cast, and mood — the four things that drift first on a long shoot day.
- Deliverable specs are complete: format, resolution, ratios, naming, selects count, usage rights, deadline.

## If a Jinn MCP connection is present

Ground the brief in the brand's real system instead of inferred taste. Climb to the highest rung your token supports.

**Rung 1 — Connected tokens (design trio present).** If `tools/list` includes the design trio, call `get_token_context` for a slug, then `get_brand_kit({ slug })` + `get_brand_design_tokens({ slug })` + `get_brand_design_md({ slug })`, plus `get_brand_dna_public({ slug })`. Set, prop, and wardrobe color direction comes from the DTCG color tokens **verbatim — never approximate a hex on set**. Logo/product-label placement in any packaging or on-pack shot follows the brand kit. Where `get_brand_design_md` conventions cover photography or imagery usage, they **override generic styling taste**.

**Rung 2 — DNA-only (trio absent, `get_brand_dna_public` works).** Call `get_token_context` → `get_brand_dna_public({ slug })`. Derive mood language and a palette direction from the personality fields below, and **label every visual choice as an unverified inference** in the brief's notes.

**Rung 3 — No token (generic-tasteful).** Use the ungrounded procedure above and add an explicit **"not brand-verified"** line to the brief.

| Source · field | Drives |
|-----------------|--------|
| `get_brand_design_tokens` — color / type / spacing (DTCG) | Set, prop, and wardrobe accent colors, verbatim hex. |
| `get_brand_kit` — logo, wordmark, brand name | Logo/label placement in any packaging or on-pack shot. |
| `get_brand_design_md` — layout & usage conventions | Any documented photography or imagery-usage rules; overrides generic taste on conflict. |
| `get_brand_dna_public` — `tonalAttributes` | The mood-in-three-words language. |
| `get_brand_dna_public` — `messagingPillars` | Which pillar the hero shot has to visually carry. |
| `get_brand_dna_public` — `tribes` / `painPoints` | Who the lifestyle shots put in frame, and what context they're shown solving. |
| `get_brand_dna_public` — `mission` / `coreValues` / `archetype` | Sanity-check that the shoot's mood matches the brand's actual character, not a generic "premium" default. |

Grounded, the brief stops guessing at "on-brand" mood adjectives and set colors, and starts using the brand's real ones. State the rung you reached in the brief's notes so the user can see the grounding did work.

Only the fields above exist on a public token — there is no competitor, ad-performance, or platform-fit data in the projection. Don't reference it or ask for it.

### Best — a Connected brand on Jinn

Once a brand is Connected, Vermeer keeps every shot — the ones a real photographer takes on this brief, and the ones Vermeer renders with AI — consistent with the brand's own shoot bible: the same continuity decisions (setting, light, who's in frame, mood) that section 6 above asks you to write down by hand, synthesized from the brand's Brand DNA and validated automatically before a render fires. ## When a call fails

Read `data.code` on the JSON-RPC error and act — the brief still ships in its ungrounded form:

- **`token_expired`** → request a fresh token: `curl -X POST https://app.jinn.works/api/agents/request-demo-token`, update `JINN_MCP_TOKEN`, retry.
- **`token_malformed`** → your agent likely sent `${JINN_MCP_TOKEN}` literally (Claude Code header bug [anthropics/claude-code#51581](https://github.com/anthropics/claude-code/issues/51581)). Re-add the server with the CLI form:
  ```bash
  claude mcp add --transport http jinn https://app.jinn.works/api/mcp \
    --header "Authorization: Bearer <token>"
  ```
- **tool error `not_found`** on any brand call → that slug isn't in your token's allowlist. Call `get_token_context` and use one of the `brand_slugs` it returns.
- **`get_brand_design_md` returns `not_found` while `get_brand_dna_public` succeeds for the same slug** → that brand simply has no design-md yet (per-brand availability), **not** a wrong slug. Proceed on the kit + tokens, fall back to generic-tasteful styling for the missing conventions, and note the gap in the brief.
- **No token / no connection** → the brief still ships in full; note it's not brand-verified and connect Jinn to ground the mood, palette, and messaging emphasis in a real brand.

## Try this now

1. **Build a full brief for a real shoot** — `Build a shoot brief for our new ceramic mug line — hero images for our PDP, Instagram feed, and email, budget-tier studio shoot, one shoot day` → a full brief: shot list by channel and ratio, lighting/mood direction, props in/out, continuity notes, and deliverable specs.
2. **Build just the shot list from deliverable channels** — `What shots do we need for a product launching on our PDP, Instagram stories, and a marketplace listing?` → a shot list mapped one-to-one to those channels and their ratios.
3. **Direct lighting and mood concretely** — `Give me concrete lighting and mood direction for a lifestyle shoot of our wool blankets, mood: cozy, honest, unpolished` → a specific light-source and time-of-day direction plus the three mood words every shot has to earn.
4. **Connected: ground props and palette in real brand colors** *(requires a Jinn token)* — `Ground this shoot brief's set colors and mood language in our brand's real design tokens` → the same brief with verbatim hex colors and tonal-attribute mood language instead of inferred taste.
